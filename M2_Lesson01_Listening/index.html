Important Words

phishing

/ˈfɪʃɪŋ/

Be careful of phishing emails that ask for your personal information.

Drill: teacher says → class repeats (×3). CCQ: Is phishing a real email from your bank? (No, it's a fake one to steal your info.)

unsecured

/ˌʌnsɪˈkjʊrd/

Free Wi-Fi at a cafe may be unsecured, so hackers can easily see your data.

Drill: teacher says → class repeats (×3). CCQ: If a network is unsecured, is it safe to use? (No.)

decode

/diːˈkoʊd/

A hacker can decode a simple password in just ten minutes.

Drill: teacher says → class repeats (×3). CCQ: What does "decode" mean? (To figure out / to break a code.)

symbols

/ˈsɪmbəlz/

A strong password has a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols like @ or #.

Drill: teacher says → class repeats (×3). Elicit examples of symbols: @, #, $, %, &.

curiosity

/ˌkjʊriˈɑːsəti/

Out of curiosity, some people click on links in strange emails — and that's how hackers get them.

Drill: teacher says → class repeats (×3). CCQ: When you feel curiosity, do you want to know more? (Yes.)

Giving a talk

Now you're going to share your own ideas.

Let's learn how to structure your talk first.

Structure Your Talk

Thesis: Say your main idea

Reasons: Give 1-2 reasons

Example: Share an example you know

Use These Words

First... say your main idea

Also... give your reasons

For example... share something you know

"Identity theft is a big problem. First... Also... For example..."

Read the example talk aloud for students. "Identity theft is a big problem. First, hackers can steal your personal information. Also, they can use it to get money or credit cards. For example, I heard about someone whose identity was stolen and the hacker opened a credit card in their name."

Prepare Your Talk

  • What is identity theft?
  • Why is it a problem?
  • Give an example you know.

Use: First...   Also...   For example...

Stage 5 · Part 2 · 2 min prep · S Students prepare quietly. Give them a minute to think and write notes. Remind them: this is just practice — don't worry about mistakes. After 2 min, ask for 2-3 volunteers to deliver their talks.
