## Let's get Started

### What do these two people have in common? What makes them different?

Notes:
Display the photo. Give students 20 seconds to look silently.
Then ask the question. Elicit 3-4 responses.
Connect responses to today's topic: What Connects Us.
- Display or refer to the photo on page 5. In pairs, Students discuss: What differences do you notice between the two people? In what ways are they the same? Is each person exactly what we expect of someone their age?.
- Feedback – elicit responses from pairs..
- In new pairs, Students tell each other about a time they disagreed with someone from a different generation. What was it about? Did you resolve it?.
- Brief whole-class feedback.
Goal: To activate interest in the theme of generational differences

## Let's Discuss

- In pairs, Students answer the questions from Exercise 5 using ideas from the article:
1. Think of one or two things you have now that you think your parents wish they had when they were your age.
2. Explain something about yourself or your life that you wish the older generation understood better.
3. Name one or two useful things you've learned from someone in an older generation.

Notes:
Stage 5 · 7 min · Ss-Ss
Goal: To discuss and respond personally to ideas from the reading
Materials: OFD 3, Student Book, pp 5-6
