Finding details

We're now going to read the story in more detail.

Let's start with some True/False questions.

They may look easy, but they can have some surprises!

True/False Strategy

"The author wrote the text to explore the generation gap and problems it can cause, and to suggest a possible solution."

Step 1: Read the statement carefully

Step 2: Work out what the question is asking

  • Did the author write about the generation gap? (Yes/No)
  • Did the author write about the problems it can cause? (Yes/No)
  • Did the author suggest a possible solution? (Yes/No)

Rule: Yes to all → TRUE · No to any → FALSE

Step 3: Find the evidence

Keywords in paras A + F: "generation gap" · "empathy" · "solution"

Step 4: Answer the question

"The author wrote the text to explore the generation gap..."

  • generation gap → "growing generation gap between people"
  • problems → "serious problems in families and workplaces"
  • solution → "only way to close the gap is through empathy"

Answer: TRUE

Paragraph Matching Strategy

"The generation gap affects people in their job because it means that people of different ages working together may have very different opinions."

Step 1: Read the statement

Find the main topic: job + people of different ages + different opinions → workplace conflict

Step 2: Find keywords

Unique phrases: "people in their job" · "different ages working together" · "different opinions"

Step 3: Scan paragraphs A–F

Look for keywords: "workplace", "different expectations", "frustration" → Paragraph B

Step 4: Confirm the match

B says: "As young employees enter companies, they may have completely different expectations... This can lead to frustration for everyone."

The whole statement matches — not just one word.

Making conclusions

Now let's think about what the writer really wants us to understand. Pay attention to the details...

Multiple Choice Strategy

"Differences can be good if we take time to understand them."

Step 1: Read all options — look at a, b, c before deciding

Step 2: Eliminate wrong answers

a) "easier for younger people" — too narrow, not main point. Eliminate.

b) "technology causes conflict" — only paragraph D. Eliminate.

Step 3: Confirm the answer

c) Matches article's main message about empathy → c is correct!
