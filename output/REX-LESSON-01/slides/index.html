Let's Read

What is a Topic Sentence?

It tells you the main idea of the paragraph.
Everything else supports it.

Topic sentence: "From tipping, to smiling, and how you eat,
there are different ways to show politeness across the world."

  • Example: tipping — expected in USA, not in Japan
  • Example: left hand — fine in Europe, unclean in India

The examples all support the topic sentence!

Exercise C1 — Topic sentences

  • Read paragraphs 2, 3, and 4 of the text.
  • Underline the topic sentence in each one.
30s. Topic sentences express the main idea. Teacher checks by discussion.

How to Match Main Ideas

Use the topic sentence. It tells you what the paragraph is about.

Para 2 topic sentence: "While languages change over time... they also change in terms of their structure and use."

Key words: languages change, structure and use

This matches: A — People use different phrases for politeness

The paragraph talks about "You're welcome" vs "No problem" — different phrases!

Finding Details

How to Scan

Look for the same words. But don't expect an exact match every time.

"...older people are likely to say ______..."

Scan Paragraph 2 for likely to say — it's an exact match here:

"Older people are more likely to say 'You're welcome'..."

Tip: Sometimes the text uses different words.
Then look for the same idea, not the exact word.

Answer: You're welcome
